Hi Stephan,


ImageOpener.<ExplicitType> openImg(...);
>

Interesting, I had not seen this syntax before. Thanks for that!

I tried it, but unfortunately, I do not think it solves the problem. You
still have to get the T from somewhere further up the chain. The compiler
cannot deduce it in a vacuum, and in the case of ImgOpener you do not know
a priori what the type of the resultant object is going to be.


In the context of Dataset which anyway erases T to RealType, you may
> also just open ImgPlus without T, suppress the warnings and on demand
> cast back.
>

Right, using raw types seemed like the easy choice, but Javac still rejects
it, similar to the error reported by Jenkins:

AnisotropicDiffusion3DExample.java:[51,32] invalid inferred types for T;
> inferred type does not conform to declared bound(s) inferred:
> java.lang.Object
> bound(s):
> java.lang.Object,net.imglib2.type.numeric.RealType<java.lang.Object>,net.imglib2.type.NativeType<java.lang.Object>
>

The code in question is already using a raw type:

Img image = createExampleImage();
>

Anyway, for now it is moot because I fixed it a different way last night:
   https://github.com/imagej/imglib/commit/42bd9efa

Dscho and I played with a few ways around the ImgOpener problem, and found
a couple of hacky solutions we are considering, but we shelved it for now
until the release is finished. More later!

Regards,
Curtis
